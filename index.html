About

Hi, I'm Artoghrul — born in Azerbaijan, raised in a Russian-speaking community through YouTube. At first, I joined the wider English-speaking internet just to improve my language skills… but I somehow became a part of it.

The internet is a strange but real place for me. It's nothing like the outside world — people can help each other deeply, or hurt each other just as much. It's full of knowledge, yet sometimes too much of it can be dangerous. It's mysterious and familiar at the same time — a living, breathing being made of countless minds, both human and AI.

Like everyone else, my life moves in waves — ups and downs, calm moments and chaos. You can see all of that inside my playlist, which I've been building for around six years. At first, it was just the pop music I heard from friends, relatives, and random places. Then came the YouTube era — songs I found in videos or recommendations. And finally, the current stage: I add everything I truly love.

My playlist has become a kind of mirror — a psychological portrait that reflects my traits, my moods, my growth. I've poured a piece of my spirit into it. I plan to preserve it forever — maybe by downloading every track and storing them on an old iPod or some little personal archive. I know the playlist by heart, even the order of many songs, though it has over 1,700 now. It's something that defines me, and helps me stay me.

I love sharing my playlist — especially those special, hidden songs that hit different. So here I am, turning it into a website to show the world the music that raised me.
